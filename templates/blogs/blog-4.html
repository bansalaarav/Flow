How Can Drone Technology Be Utilised In The Achievement Of The United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals? Original by Aarav Bansal, Edited by Team FLOW 11 November 2021

As said in our previous articles, drone technology, which originated in military applications, is now widely used for commerical, professional, industrial and private purposes. Not only are drones eco-friendly gadgets, but they are also time and cost-efficient. In this post, we are going to see which industries drones dominate in these days and how these robots can be used to help achieve some of the SDGs set by the United Nations.


The 12 sustainable development goals set by the UN
Which Industries Do Drones Influence The Most These Days?

Drones can help in various sectors, some of the most promising ones being delivery, agriculture, e-commerce and health care. Hence, in the coming years, the adoption of drone technology will undoubtedly turn into a great trend as more and more industries embrace the technology. In the image shown below, we can see the photography and real estate industries were some of the most popular industries for drones in 2015 (and still are today). Let us now see some of the potential usages of drone technology.


Top industries influenced by the drone technology in 2015
How Can Drones Help In The Agricultural Sector?

Drone technology can help in the agricultural sector, and thus in the achievement of SDG 2 - Zero Hunger, in a number of ways, for example, to survey farm fields, to ensure product delivery and to spray pesticides. Rather than spraying the entire field, the pesticide can be delivered to the right spot, only in the quantity needed, which means reduction in pesticides used, reduction in collateral damage to wildlife and also enhanced cost-efficiency.


A drone in Japan

The case of Japan provides a good illustration for such applications of the drone technology. Since the 1970s, this country has accumulated significant experience in this sphere. Nearly 2,000 UAVs are being used in Japan today for agricultural spraying and planting operations. Furthermore, drones can connect farmers to markets and thus ensure that everyone has access to affordable nutritious food. Let us now see how drones can help with scientific research.

How Can Drones Help With Scientific Research

Apart from the agricultural usage of drones, they can also help in the achievement of many other other sustainable development goals by helping scientists with their research. Drones can withstand extreme conditions and are expendable, which makes them perfect for research of diseases, pollution levels in regions with extreme weather conditions, radioactive areas and so on.


A scientist in the Arctic region taking the help of a drone for research
Let's Conclude...

It is evident that drones are going to make a great contribution to the achievement of the SDGs. Drone technology not only has a promising robust influence in the agricultural sector, but in a number of other sectors.
